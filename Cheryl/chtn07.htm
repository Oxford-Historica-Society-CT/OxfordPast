John F. CHATFIELD

JOHN F. CHATFIELD (1752-1837):  John was one of those who marched "For the relief of Boston in the Lexington Alarm" in Apr 1775.  A little later he enlisted in the 7th Co., 6th Reg., and served until Dec 19th, when the company was disbanded.
    Administration on the Estate of John Chatfield of Oxford was granted Jul 13, 1837 to Russell Chatfield and Benjamin M. Collins of Bethany.  Distribution was made to David K. Chatfield, Russell Chatfield, Pulaski Chatfield, Hannah Hull, the heirs of Henry Chatfield, deceased, Polly Finch [French?], the heirs of Sarahan (maybe Sarah Ann) [sic] Hyde, deceased, and the heirs of Pamelia Perry, deceased.
     New Haven probate records Vol. 46, pg. 470.
CENSUS 1790:  Derby, New Haven Co., CT; Head of Household "John Chatfield".   Males:  over 16 - 1; under 16 - 4; Females:  (age not given) - 3; pg. 94.
Genealogy.com (LLC), Broderbund (FTJ), Vol. 73, Tree 0201.
