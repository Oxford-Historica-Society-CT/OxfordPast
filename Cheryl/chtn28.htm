John , Ia CHATFIELD

JOHN CHATFIELD (1475-0000):  John was the son of Thomas Chatfield, b. about 1475, and alive in 1523 when he appears in a subsidy.  He was of Bedyles in the parish of Ditchling according the the Visitations of Sussex, which also states that he md. Alice (Stapley), dau. of Richard of Twineham, co. Sussex, England.
    He is evidently the John Chatfield, Sr. who was assessed in the 1523 subsidy in the Hundred of Street, goods xxx s.
Lay Subsidies for co. Sussex:  Preserved in Public Record Office, London, England.
"Genealogical Research in England".
BY:  Cheryl (Chatfield) Thompson.

William CLARK

WILLIAM CLARK, born 5 Mar 1795 in MILFORD, NEW HAVEN, Connecticut. He married MINERVA HIGGINS 23 Oct 1817. She was born 29 Aug 1779, the daughter of SYLVESTER HIGGINS and COMFORT LYMAN. WILLIAM died 6 Aug 1862.

Divine I CHATFIELD

DIVINE CHATFIELD, I (1787-1860):  Was Capt. of the 8th Co., 2nd Reg., CT Militia, in Sep 1819.  He became a member of Christ Church at Quaker Farms, in 1833.

Isaiah CHATFIELD

MARRIAGE NOTE:
    Isaiah md. Grace in 1820 and both lived into the 1880's, with the info in his will listing his wife as Grace, I believe it is doubtful he married Mary Ann Holbrook -- See Isaiah's brother Bennett who md. Mary Ann Holbrook -- Nov 01, 1830 in Derby, New Haven, CT.  
                                                      The will of Isaiah Chatfield:
    "Second:  I give unto my wife Grace Chatfield all the personal Estate of which I may die possessed to be her own forever, subject to the above bequest."  (The above bequest referred to debts and funeral expenses.)
    "Third:  I give unto my wife Grace Chatfield the use of all the real estate of which I may die possessed to use as her own for and during her natural wife."
    "Fourth:  I give unto my dau. Caroline Lum, wife of William A. Lum and subject to the above bequests, one certain piece or tract of land known as Lake Meadow --- to be her own forever."
    "Fifth:  I also give to my dau. Caroline Lum all the rest and remainder of my estate if any there be after paying to my son John Chatfield, One Dollar to be his own forever."
    The will of Isaiah, from the Town of Oxford, New Haven, CT, dated Dec 12, 1874 indicates the same message in reference to their son John H. as the will of his wife.  Was their a breach of affection between parents and their son John H. Chatfield?  Or maybe he had already received his share?
