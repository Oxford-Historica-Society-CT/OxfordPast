Russell CHATFIELD

RUSSELL CHATFIELD (1787-1878):  He was said to have been the oldest Mason in the state. He was made a Mason in SC, it is thought in 1809, making him sixty-nine years a Mason.  He was a mason by trade as well as a member of the order, having assisted in building the prison at Wethersfield and many other public buildings.
     In his younger days he worked at his trade in CT in the summer, and went south in the winter.  He was blind the last four years of his life, but his memory was good.  His wife d. about a year before, the pair having lived together over sixty-five years.  The funeral was attended by the members of Shepherd Lodge of Naugatuck.
"The Chatfield Family", by William C. Sharpe, 1896, Seymour, New Haven, CT.

NELSON NEWTON BEECHER (1842-1863):  Private, Co. G., 27th Reg., CT Volunteer Infantry.  He d. at Annapolis on Jun 24, 1863.  Sometimes referred to as Newton Nelson Beecher.
