Dennis Dabney [adptd] DAMAN

DENNIS DABNEY DAMAN (1941-1960):  Dennis was an adopted child.  He d. while attending West Point Prep School.

Joel Briggs I DAMAN

JOEL BRIGGS DAMAN, I (1791-1849):  Tombstone says he was 56 years old when he d., but according to birth date registered in Ware, Hampshire, MA, he was 58 years old when he died.

Abram Reuben DAMAN

ABRAM REUBEN DAMAN (1839-0000):  In the 1850 CENSUS in LaFayette, Goshen, IL, he was with Charles Lake and Elizabeth Ann (Davis) Lake.  These two were 37 and 25 respectfully and Reuben was 11.  Elizabeth was b. in IN, Charles in VT, and Reuben in IL.  mg. records from Stark, IL show that Charles and Elizabeth were md. Oct 18, 1843, by Edward Trickle, J. P.
     I believe Reuben moved to MO after he grew up.  There is a record of a man named A. R. Daman in the CIVIL WAR records.

Thomas "Tommy" DAMAN

THOMAS "TOMMY" DAMAN (1915-1985):  Graduated from Renwick School.  Vice Commander of Adams Post No. 119, American Legion of Humboldt.  Member of VFW Post No. 5240 of Dakota City.  Loved to fish and bowl.  Donated his eyes to Lions Eye Bank.  Reunited with sister, Margaret, after 56 years.

Matilda M. DAMAN

MATILDA M. (DAMAN) LANTON (1841-0000):  Matilda Daman lived with Benjamin S. & Sabrina Hilliard, Lafayette, Stark, IL after her mother, Sarah (Chatfield) Daman, died.  I believe Sabrina (Chatfield) Hilliard may have been the dau. of Isaac and Sabra Chatfield.  NOTE: Belief  is CORRECT.
    Listed in 1850 CENSUS as living with Benjamin L. & Sabrina Killian, Stark Co. IL.  Age 9.
    Copy of mg. Cert. and note signed by her brother permitting the mg. as Matilda was orphaned at only 11 years old.  Found possible link via William Lanton --- Genealogy.com (LLC), Broderbund (FTJ), Vol. 01, Tree 2547?

Francis Elmo DAMAN

FRANCIS ELMO DAMAN (1883-1939):  In 1910 worked for Starch & Corn Syrup Mfg. Co. in Keokuk, IA.  At that time he lived at 1004 Reid.
    Francis Elmo and Birdie are living with father, Francis Marion in Keokuk in 1910.  Francis and
Birdie either gave away or lost custody of all or most all of their children.  The children were left in an
orphanage in Toledo, Tama Co., IA.  Francis E. & Birdie lived in CO and MO for a while.  Francis Elmo was called Elmer in Thomas' obit.
Keokuk City Directory.

Wayne [b. Daman-adptd] HOLLAND

WAYNE [b. Daman-adopted Holland] DAMAN-HOLLAND (1900's-0000):  Wayne was adopted. His new adopted name was HOLLAND.

Sunshine DAMAN

SUNSHINE DAMAN (1900's-0000):  "Sunshine" may have been a nickname, if so, her given name eludes me.  Child d. at five.  She was walking with brother, and was hit by a car.

Paul E. [b. Daman-adptd] TRIGG

PAUL E. (b. Daman-adopted Trigg) DAMAN-TRIGG (1918-0000):    Live in an orphanage in Toledo, IA for many years before adopted by family -- name changed to TRIGG.

George Herbert I DAMAN

GEORGE HERBERT DAMAN (1893-0000):  George lived with Francis Marion in Keokuk, IA in 1910, and worked in a Starch and Corn Syrup factory at the time.  He later lived in Oakland, CA where he died.
