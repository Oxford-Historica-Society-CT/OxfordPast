Leonard Minor CURRY

LEONARD MINERS CURRY (1867-1898):  Leonard Miners Curry had very dark gray, almost light brown eyes and light brown hair.  He went to school until he was between 16 and 17 years of age, and was then in the second grade in Lincoln Grammar School.  He went though the second grade and then took a notion he wanted to learn printing.  He worked at printing, with Mr. Brown, for several months but did not like printing as well as he thought he would.  So his father got him a place with Mr. Hammond to learn watch making and jewelry repairing.  He went to work with Mr., Hammond at #4 6th Street in Oct 1883.
         Leonard was named Leonard for his grandfather Kimball and Miners for Capt. Thomas Miners, his Grandmother Curry's sister Caroline's husband.
         As Mr. Hammond did not give Leonard as good a chance to learn the trade as he promised to,
his father took him away from him and got him a place with James Kite on Mission street in 1885.
         Leonard md. Mary Purdy who was b. in Jackson, Amador Co., CA.  The wedding ceremony was performed by Rev. Dr. Case, pastor of the Central M.E. Church in San Francisco.
         His death is not recorded and no information is available.  On Aug 8, 1898 he was in Chicago, where an attorney, Morris Frisch, wrote to C. F. Curry saying his brother was attempting to get a rebate on a lost train ticket.  He was never heard from afterwards.  Disappeared after divorcing his wife.  
          His son was raised by his brother, Charles.  Jack London was a family friend and offered to adopt the son.  No other information on him.

Charles Forrest I CURRY

CHARLES FORREST CURRY, I (1858-1830):  Secretary of State of CA, 1888-1906.  Also in Congress for 22 yrs.

Anna Minnie CURRY

ANNE MINNIE (CURRY) PETERSON (0000-1948):  Her obituary, May 1, 1948:
    Mrs. Anne Minnie Peterson, a long time resident of Sacramento and mother of a former deputy attorney general of CA, will be bur. Monday in the Mountain View Cem. in Oakland.
    She d. yesterday in the home of her niece, Florence Curry of 2701 G. Street, after a short illness.  A native of Mineral Point, WI, she came to Sacramento in 1899 and had lived here since that time.  Her residence was at her niece's home.
    Her son, the late Emmett J. Peterson, d. in 1935 while serving as deputy attorney general of CA.  A brother, Charles F. Curry, was a representative of the Third Sacramento Congressional District for 18 years and for 12 years was CA secretary of state.
    She was an active member of the D.A.R. and at one time was the regent of the Continental Chapter of the organization in Washington, DC.
    In addition to Miss Curry, she leaves her nephews, C. F. Curry, Jr. of Sacramento and Leonard C. Curry of San Francisco and her other nieces, Juanity Curry and Mrs. Homer Morgan of San Francisco.  Her husband, the late James Peterson, d. in 1903. [Sacramento Bee].
    She was admitted to the D.A.R., National #150962, on Dec 17, 1919.
