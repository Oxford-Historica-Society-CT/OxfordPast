Emeline Bassett CHATFIELD

The National Society of the Daughters of the American Revolution Volume 60
page 51
Mrs. Emmeline B. Chatfield Sherwood.
DAR ID #: 59146
Born in Derby, Conn.
Wife of Albert F. Sherwood.
[p.51] Descendant of Abner Tibbils and Samuel Morehouse, of Connecticut.
Daughter of Charles R. Chatfield and Rhoda Gerard, his wife.
Granddaughter of Henry Chatfield and Amanda Tibbils, his wife; Azel Gerard and Rhoda Morehouse, his wife.
Gr-granddaughter of Abner Tibbils and Clara —, his wife; Samuel Morehouse and Anna Betts, his wife.
Abner Tibbils (1757-1830) enlisted, 1778, from Haddam in Capt. Jairus Wilcox's company, Baldwin's regiment, and served three years as an artificer. He was a pensioner. He died in Derby.
Also No. 7408.
Samuel Morehouse (1762-1830) was a volunteer when Tryon invaded the colony, and his widow received a pension for his service. He was born in Norwalk.
Also No. 20832.

Daniel , Ib CHATFIELD

Pension Application #S37830:  REV WAR CT Line.  Application dated Mar 7, 1818, Litchfield Co., CT, soldier resident of Oxford, New Haven, CT age 58.  Soldier had lived at Oxford, New Haven, CT at enlistment.  On Feb 28, 1826 was a resident of Columbia Co., NY living with his only surviving son, Elias.  On Oct 28, 1831 soldier stated they moved to Oxford, New Haven, CT to live with wife's relatives.
    CENSUS 1790:  Derby, New Haven Co., CT; Head of Household "Dan Chatfield".   Males:  over 16 - 1; under 16 - 0; Females:  (age not given) - 4; pg. 94.

HUNTINGTON BURYING-PLACES, STRATFORD, CONNECTICUT
Dan Chatfield, Born 1761, died 1845.
A veteran soldier of the war for American Independence.

Sarah Chatfield, daughter of Dan Chatfield, and wife of Lemuel Summers,
Born Aug. 28, 1788,. Died Jan. 8, 1873.

David Chatfield, Born June 8, 1794, died June 30, 1836.

Nancy, his wife, born April 6, 1796, died Aug. 25, 1879; daughter of Dan Chatfield.

Erected by David H. Chatfield, son of David Chatfield.

John E. Chatfield Died Jan. 5, 1877. Aged 66 Years.

John , IIc Lt. CHATFIELD

LT. JOHN CHATFIELD, IIb LT (1691-1793:  John was appointed Ensign of the company or train band, in the parish of Oxford, New Haven, CT  in Oct 1743, and in Oct 1750 he was appointed Lt. of the Second Company at the town of Derby, New Haven, CT.
