Wales CHATFIELD

WALES CHATFIELD (1837-1914):  Wales owned and occupied the homestead which belonged to his father, his grandfather, and his great-grandfather, in Oxford, New Haven, CT .  He was one of the most respected citizens of the town which had been the home of his ancestors from 1684 or a little earlier.
"The Chatfield Family", by William C. Sharpe, 1896, Seymour, New Haven, CT.

David King CHATFIELD

DAVID KING CHATFIELD (1783-1853):  Testator of Sep 10, 1853, five days before his death.  Bequeaths to:  "my wife Mary Chatfield" --- "at the decease of my wife Mary Chatfield, all my property real and personal the use of which is given to her during her natural life as above specified be given to my son Wales Chatfield to be his and his heirs forever on condition that he shall pay to my dau.'s.  To Eliza the wife of Charles Tomlinson the sum of One Hundred Dollars.  And also to my dau. Maria Chatfield the like sum of One Hundred Dollars."   Was a member of Christ Church Quaker Farms.
