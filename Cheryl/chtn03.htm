Zalmon S. PECK

Zalmon S. Peck resided in the South Centre District of the town of Newton, was in his active years one of the prominent public men.  Born May 22, 1812, he is descended from Joseph Peck of Milford, the line of descent being through Joseph, of the second generation, Ephraim, Henry, Capt. Zalmon and Ezekiel.  During the CIVIL WAR he served as the enrolling and drafting officer of Newton.  He was made postmaster of the town under President Lincoln's first term, and held the position for twenty-six years, being out for two years (from 1867 to 1869) under the Johnson administration.  He was a very kind and obliging gentleman, well preserved, and an interesting conversationalist.  Time seemed to have dealt kindly with him.

Henry S. PECK

Henry S. Peck was reared and schooled in Newton.  He served in the CIVIL WAR, and soon thereafter entered the employ of the Waterbury Clock Company, at Waterbury, New Haven, CT.  He was with them some twenty or more years, much of which time he spent in Chicago looking after their Western trade.
    Later he was located in Waterbury, New Haven, CT as the secretary and treasurer of The Brass City Lumber Company.  He was vice-president of the Equitable Finance Company of Baltimore, MD.  He md. Isabell Boughton, of Waterbury, New Haven, CT.

Austin L. PECK

Austin L. Peck is not bur. in the Newton Cem.  Since he md. a lady from Hartford, Hartford, CT is it fair to assume that he may be bur. somewhere there; or possibly MI?
    Austin's boyhood was spent in Newton.  On the breaking out of the CIVIL WAR he entered the United States service as a member of CT VI, Co. C, but was made a prisoner of war at Brashear City, LA.     Returning home after an honorable war record, he soon became active in the business world.  He located in Hartford, Hartford, CT and was one of a firm of Andrews & Peck, wholesale dealers in doors, windows, etc.  He was president of The Brass City Lumber Company of Waterbury, and also treasurer of The Big Rapids Door and Blind Manufacturing Company of Big Rapids, MI.  Upon reading the above it would seem possible that he came to MI and stayed.
