Oliver I CHATFIELD

OLIVER CHATFIELD (1730-1788):  Oliver was a soldier in the REV WAR.  He enlisted May 19, 1775, and was discharged Dec 20, 1775.  In Jun, 1776, he re-enlisted in Capt. Nathaniel Johnson's Co., Col. William Douglas' Regiment, 5th Battalion, and served until Dec 25, 1776.  He later served in the company with Lieut. Jabez Pritchard.  
     Oliver was one of those that marched to "The Relief at Boston in the Lexington Alarm" in Apr 1775, under the command of Capt. John Thompson's Co., Col. David Wooster's Reg..  D.A.R. says Oliver served as a Pvt. under Capt.'s Thompson and Johnson; and Col.'s Wooster and Douglas, CT Troops.  He was b. 1730 in Derby, New Haven, CT and d. at Oxford, New Haven, CT.  His descendants are eligible for membership in D.A.R.
    The will of Oliver, dated Jun 30, 1788 and proved Dec 15, 1788, names his wife Zerviah, his sons Benajah, Isaac, Eli, Oliver, Dan, Abijah, and Joseph.  Also, his dau.'s Patience Gilbert and Elizabeth Chatfield.  
D.A.R. application, 1919.
