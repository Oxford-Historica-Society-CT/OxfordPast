Jennette BEACH

Chatfield family records identify her as the daughter of Samuel Beach, whose will names only two sons, stating that his other children had already been provided for during his lifetime.

John , Ic CHATFIELD

JOHN CHATFIELD, Ib (1661-1747): Anna (Harger) Chatfield md. John Chatfield on Feb 15, 1684, when she was fourteen years of age.  From them were descended Amos Bronson Alcott, and his dau. Louisa May Alcott, the well-known authoress.
    The will of John Chatfield, dated Apr 12, 1739, and proved Mar 7, 1747/8, names his wife Anna, his sons John, Samuel, Ebenezer, and Solomon, and his dau.'s Mary Beats, Sarah Davis, and Abigail Johnson.  
"The Chatfield Family", Naugatuck Valley, CT;
"Barbour Collection", Guilford VR, Vol. A, pg. 63.

"...of Derby, died, and his will dated, April 12, 1737.  Children:  his sons, John and Samuel, had their share of his estate by previous deeds, and Ebenezer most of his share.  Solomon had his house, barn and land where John then lived.  His daughters were Mary, Sarah Davis and Abigail Johnson.  Widow Anna Chatfield.  Chatfield has two coats of arms." p.551, Hinman

Steiner says John was one of the first settlers of Derby.
