Dear Cheryl,
    I've been thumbing through L.W. Case's book,THE GOODRICH FAMILY in AMERICA, Chicago: Fergus Publishing Company, 1889.  Now, I must caution you about its accuracy; actually, it has many, too many errors, basically because a researcher was hired and Case wasn't much of an editor.  Despite the claim that it was placed in his hands "for final revision, rearrangement, and completion, and the work has passed through the press under his supervision", the mistakes are rather obvious.  I'll give you a case in point:  my gggrandfather, Collins W. Goodrich, married Mary Ann Sackrider in Swanton, VT.on Jan. 26, 1823; she died 10 months and 8 days later.  Case credits her with all five children!!!  She had no children.  Collins married the second time in 1829 to Mary Van Duzee. It was they who were my gggrandparents!  Their five children were born in 1831 (Cordelia), 1833 (Abigail, my ggrandmother), 1835 (Minerva), 1836 (Marion) and 1842 (James V.). The Vermont 1830 and 1850 censuses prove
this.
        Your line from your first ancestor William descends through  John, Jacob, Elijah, Daniel, Daniel, Calvin Pierce Goodrich, born Oct. 20, 1817, Married Delia (sic) F. Price, Feb. 27, 1842; residence, Guttenburg, Iowa. Their son Frank (sic) was the sixth of the seven children, and his birth is listed as April 13, 1856, and a marriage to Lena V. Merz took place January 4, 1882.  Only one child is listed: Andrew C. born Oct. 29, 1886.  (Because of the publication date, there is nothing more on Andrew and it's possible
there were more children and more marriages for Calvin.)  Calvin's mothers name was Laura Pierce; hence, his middle name and, possibly, that of his son's.)
   If you would like to have a copy of the pages from which I got the above information, please send your home address via e-mail, and I'll xerox them for you.
    Our connection is that your ancestor John Goodrich was an older brother of my ancestor, David Goodrich; therefore, we are cousins, albeit distant ones at that.
     Incidentally, an excellent source for the Goodrich family is Stewart Goodrich.  He has done a whale of a job researching the Goodrich family, and I am sure he could help you.  His e-mail address is stugood@worldnet.att.net.  
     He has been a tremendous help for me, and you'll find he also thinks Case did a lousy job.  (The Mormons took Case's "word" for his material and repeated the errors I told you about above re my family. I have found they are neither reliable nor particularly truthful about their records and claims!!!)
      Hope what I have given you will be of help and not a repeat of your own hard labors.
      Much luck, cuz, and do let me know if you want copies.             Gerry

Cheryl,
    I'm sorry it has taken me so long to answer.  I have been snowed under at work.  From the info you sent the questions that come to mind are:
    Have you followed them in the census records?  If there was a change in wife you should be able to pick it up at some point.
    There are city directories (if he was in the city).  They start at 1856 and go to present day.  They are not there for every year.  In the early ones it is just the head of house but in later years they will include the wife's first name.
    What about marriage records?  Have you checked those.  They are on microfilm at the Carnegie Stout Public Library.  Early ones have just the bare bones of info.  Later ones will often say if it is a first or second marriage.
    Do you have a time period of when they were in Dubuque?  That would tell you which of the above might be helpful.
    There are divorce records supposedly at the Court House.  I have never seen them but there are supposed to be down there.  
     I have the index to Probate for Dubuque County at it does have two listings for Sarah D. Seymour.  One in 1883 Book 37 page 441 and one in 1883 Book 45 Page 445.  The second entry is marked Deceased.  The first is marked "Inc."  If this is William A.'s wife, it might have something in there about Mary Elizabeth.
    Have you checked the cemetery records to see where Enos and Mary are buried?  Are they next to each other?  
     Hope some of this helps.  I really don't know much about the Spangler's.  That was my husband's side of the family and haven't even started on that.  Kathy Spangler-Dubuque LOOK-UP
