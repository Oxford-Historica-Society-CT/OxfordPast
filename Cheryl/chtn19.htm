Arthur Egbert DAMAN

ARTHUR EGBERT DAMAN (1880-1958):  He joined the service (army) before mg. to Lillian (it is said he md. her and then left for service and she didn't see him for a year.).  They lived in Canton, MO at the time.
     Jun 13, 1898 - Oct 16, 1989:  Pvt., Co. A, 5th Vol.;
    Mar 22, 1899 - Mar 21, 1905:  Pvt., Co. F, 4th Cavalry, Jefferson Barracks, MO;
    1903 - Leavenworth, KS;
    1906 San Francisco, CA;
     1923 Philippines;
    1928 Lansing, MI;
     ?   Raliegh, NC;
    ?   TX.
    He served in WW I, Spanish American War, and WW II.
     Arthur "Babe" Daman and A. E. Daman went to NY to visit  the Widow of Noah, the Widow of the last surviving CIVIL WAR veteran and a Cousin of A. E. Daman.
     At time of his death, Arthur was at home - 1811 Reynolds Blvd., El Paso, TX.

Lillian Matilda RICHARDS

In 1927, Leonard & wife, Eula, alone with their son, Jean, went to the funeral of his mother.
          They rode a train from Monroe, MI.  That was the last time Leonard was at the grave site until 1992.  In 1992, his son, Leonard, went to visit the grave site with his dau., Martha, and Martha's husband, Ken Wade.   The gravestone was engraved with both Lillian's and her husband, Arthur's name; but Arthur was bur. by son, Eli, in TX.
          The grave is in a nice location under a flowering shade tree in the Fort Leavenworth's Veteran's Cem. on North side of town.

Francis Marion DAMAN

FRANCIS MARION DAMAN (1847-1927):  Two records show he was b. in 1844.
1844-1847 b. ** Family letter indicates Francis lied about his age at 14 in order to join Merchant          Marines.  This may account for the discrepancy in ages.  Mg. and pension records show     he   was b. Aug 6, 1844.  
1858- According to affidavit signed by John Marsh, Francis Marion was in Greensburg, MO up until the   time he joined the Navy in 1858.
1864-1865 Union Navy.  He served aboard Susquehanna, Grand Gulf, and NC.  While taking part in the      siege of Fort Fisher, the last sea battle of the CIVIL WAR, he fell from the crows nest onto the      deck and broke both hips.
1866 voyage to London from NY.
1866-1878 ??Working with James McGray on fishing fleet.  Barrington, Nova Scotia.
Oct 5, 1875 - Md. Angeline McGray.
1878- according to affidavit signed by John Marsh, this is the year Francis M. returned to MO from Nova     Scotia.  The affidavit was also signed by a Mr. T. H. Welch. -- on Dec 21, 1889.  John Marsh     was 63 at the time.  Francis's pension records say he was b. at Lafayette, Stark, IL in   1850.  There is and never was any LaFayette Co. in IL.  Both Hilliard and Lakes lived and are   bur. in LaFayette.
1880 - He lived in Greenburg, MO next door to John Marsh.  Francis was listed as Frank and his wife as    Fanny on the 1880 CENSUS records in Greensboro, MO.  There is no doubt that this is Francis     as my grand-father, Arthur, is listed as his son.  The niece, Anna, probably is Reuben A.           Daman's dau. who is also listed at the Daman's house in the same CENSUS.
1890 - He was in Canton, MO and had a Baring, MO post office.  
1900 - Edina, MO -- At sometime he lived here.
1910 - Francis Marion and Angeline lived in Keokuk, IA with some sons.  The records here show he      was b. in IN.  Could he have been b. in LaFayette in instead of LaFayette, IL?
     Angeline & Francis lived in Keokuk, IA most of their later years according to their grandson,  Leonard Daman.
1915-1921 Angeline d. sometime between these dates.*(probably in Keokuk, IA)
Sep 12, 1921 Leavenworth, KS -- Veterans Home.              
1926 - Longmont, CO with son, George and his wife, Nola.
Dec 1927 Francis d. Oakland, CA -- bur. in Longmont, CO  (with Angeline?).  Angeline could have been bur. in Keokuk, IA.

Frances Angeline <> MCGRAY

ANGELINE (McGRAY) DAMAN (1851-0000):  1915-1921 Angeline d. sometime between 1915 and 1921 -- probably in Keokuk, IA.  I (LMD) believe she is bur. in Longmont, CO, because that is where her husband, Francis, was bur.

George Herbert DAMAN

GEORGE HERBERT DAMAN (1905-1987):  Received Citizens' Military Training Camps' Military Training Cert. for attending the Basic Red course of Instruction (Infantry Branch) from Aug 1 to Aug 30, 1923 at Fort Leavenworth, KS.
     Was Private 1st class and Corporal in the MI National Guard.  He was promoted to Corporal Dec 7, 1926 at Detroit, Wayne, MI.
     In 1910 George lived at 5th and Clark St., Denver, CO and in 1920 he lived at Kearny Ave., Ft.  Leavenworth, KS.
    d. of Respiratory arrest due to urosepsis and general debilitation due to Parkinsons disease, at Chelsea United Methodist Nursing Home.
