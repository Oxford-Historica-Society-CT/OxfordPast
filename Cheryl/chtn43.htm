Mary Louise GOLDSMITH

The National Society of the Daughters of the American Revolution Volume 83 page 155
Mrs. Mary Goldsmith Chatfield.
DAR ID Number: 82395
Born in Waterbury, Conn.
Wife of Benjamin Chatfield.
Descendant of Judah Frisbie, of Connecticut.
Daughter of Andrew Welton Goldsmith (b. 1841) and Christine Somers (b. 1844), his wife, m. 1868.
Granddaughter of David Somers (1808-60) and Almira Frisbie (1812-77), his wife, m. 1830.
Gr-granddaughter of David Frisbie (1782-1837) and Leva Hall (1783-1837), his wife, m. 1805.
Gr-gr-granddaughter of Judah Frisbie and Hannah Baldwin (1746-1829), his wife, m. 1779.
Judah Frisbie (1744-1817) enlisted, 1775, in Capt. Phineas Porter's company, 1st regiment, under General Wooster, who had charge of the Northern Department. He was born and died in Wolcott, Conn.
Also No. 54632.

Albert Isiah CHATFIELD

ALBERT I. CHATFIELD (1846-1917):  The testator of 1917.  His will names his sons "Benjamin Chatfield, of Waterbury" and "Gerard Chatfield, of the city, county and State of NY", --- "share and share alike."  In addition he bequeaths to "my granddaughter Rhoda G. Chatfield" --- "and to my granddaughter Elizabeth T. Chatfield", each to receive "the sum of Five Hundred Dollars, ($500)."  He makes no mention of any wives.

Florence Electa WARNER

FLORENCE E. (WARNER) CHATFIELD (1800's-0000):  The will of Florence W. Chatfield bequeaths to "my husband, Albert I. Chatfield", . "my brother Arthur L. Warner, or if not living, his wife Jennie D. Warner", --- "my brother Horace S. Warner  --- my interest in the real estate in Thomaston, CT formerly the property of my late father Jonathan M. Warner."


The National Society of the Daughters of the American Revolution Volume 42
page 66
Mrs. Florence Electa Warner Chatfield.
DAR ID #: 41167
Born in Plymouth, Connecticut.
Wife of Albert I. Chatfield.
Descendant of Ebenezer Seeley, of Connecticut.
Daughter of Jonathan Marsha Warner and Emily Virginia Seeley, his wife.
Granddaughter of Lloyd Seeley and Eliza Staples, his wife.
Gr.-granddaughter of Ebenezer Seeley and Anna Coley, his wife.
Ebenezer Seeley, (1761-1842), enlisted as fifer at the age of fifteen. He was placed on the pension roll 1832 of Fairfield County as private in the Connecticut Continental line. He was born in Fairfield; died in Weston.
