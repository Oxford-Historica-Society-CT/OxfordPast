1882-01-05_Seymour-Record

OXFORD

Rev. Mr. Pardee preached and celebrated the Holy Communion in St. Peter's Church last Sunday morning.

The Sunday-school Christmas entertainments both passed off pleasantly. On account of the storm on Thursday that of St. Peter's was postponed until Frday evening.

A special town meeting of Monday was adjourned till Monday, the 16th 1 p.m.

Rev. Mr. Swan will hold service in Bowers Hill school-house next Sunday afternoon at 3:30 o'clock.

Dr. E. B. Fairchild, who was obliged on account of ill-health to relinquish a very extensive and lucrative practice a few years since, is about to re-establish in the dentistry in New Haven, having leased pleasant accommodation on the second floor of the Townsend Bank building, corner of Orange and Chapel streets, Dr F. will try to please all who may extend their patronage.

A man named William Bailey, friendless, as far as the selectmen are definitely informed, died in a coal-hut Tuesday night the 3rd inst Mr. Potwine performed the last rites at the Quaker Farms cemetery.

SOUTHFORD

There was a party at Henry Wheeler's new building near the depot last Friday evening. The principal attraction of the evening was dancing. There was good music and a supper was provided by the ladies.

There was no preaching at the house of Samuel Tuttle last evening as was expected, and a prayer meeting was held.

 

P. T. Barnum announces that he will employ all curious specimens of the human race, including giants, dwarfs, fat people and freaks of nature for his great show Parties interested should write inclosing photos to Barnum, Bailey and Hutchinson, 40 Bond St., N.Y.
