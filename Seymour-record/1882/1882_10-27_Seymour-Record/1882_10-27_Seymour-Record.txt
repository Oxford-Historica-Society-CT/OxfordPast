1882_10-27_Seymour-Record

OXFORD

Only six of the thirty entitled to be made, took the freeman's oath on Wednesday.  Next Monday will be the last opportunity.

Rev Mr. Snow will now resume services in the different districts, commencing at Christian street next Sunday afternoon at 3.30 o'clock.

On account of the weather being stormy last Thursday the Society at Mrs. Candee's was continued until Friday. The receipts were over $13.

Win. 0. Davis went out cooning last Saturday night and in addition to two coons, captured a fine specimen of a fox.

The Ladies Society St. Peters church will meet at the residence of Mrs. C. H. Butler on Thursday afternoon and evening of next week. If stormy it will be held on the following day, as usual.

The democratic caucus for the nomination of candidates for representative and Judge of Probate is called at the town hall on Saturday evening, Nov. 4th. 

SOUTHFORD.

William Oat in is having a new house built a short distance above the store. The work is progressing finely and will soon be completed.

The work on the new paper mill is progressing finely. When completed it will be an imposing edifice, and will give employment to a large force of workmen.

SQUANTUCK.

First frost of the season Friday morning, very light.

Mr. and Mrs. Clemons started on a trip to Providence and Boston on Monday. They will visit the New England Mechanics' Fair and expect to be gone about a week.

We hear that a newly married couple living not far above here have parted before the honey moon was fairly over.

P. and E. Tomlinson have been putting in an iron shaft in place of the wooden shaft in their overshot water wheel. Chas. Hull has been doing the job and it looks substantially done.

Eli Gillette is having his shop moved and joined on to his house for a kitchen, and is otherwise improving his house.

Mrs. Tolles is gaining slowly and hopes to be with her Sunday school class next Sunday.

Miss Alice Tomlinson is very feeble and is gradually wasting away.

From different sections of the state comes word there was light frost last Friday night, checked by the cloudiness toward morning, but sufficient to justify the prognostication of "Old Probabilities."
