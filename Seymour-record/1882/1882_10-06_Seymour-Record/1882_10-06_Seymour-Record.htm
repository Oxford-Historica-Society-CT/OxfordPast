1882_10-06_Seymour-Record.

 

OXFORD.

 

Nathan J. Wilcoxson, who had been in feeble health for several months, from dropsical difficulty, but apparently more comfortable of late, died suddenly on Saturday evening, while sitting by the table reading, having only a few moments before eaten a hearty supper.

The deceased had over-reached the ripe age of 86 years.

 

He was born in Newtown, Conn., and has been a resident of this town 55 years. He has held the offices of judge of probate, town clerk, school visitor and other offices of honor and trust within the gift of the people of the town, including a term in the General Assembly in 1844, when he also acted as journalist of the House, it then being the custom to select the clerks from the members.

 

The writer has many times been amused to hear related the circumstance of his selection at that time, far different and more honorable than the heated strife for the positions within the gift of either House nowadays.

 

The funeral of the deceased was largely attended from the Congregational church of which he had long been a member, Rev. F. E. Snow officiating. Over fifty Masons were present to pay the last tribute to a brother, the deceased being a member of Morning Star Lodge, No. 47, F. & A. M. He received the third degree March 21st, 1826, and consequently had been a Mason 56 years.

 

SQUANTUCK

 

Arthur Clemons shot a bald eagle at Indian Well last Saturday, which measured 6 ft. 4 inches from tip to tip of wings.

 

Peter and Edwin Tomlinson's circular saw came to a full stop from the breaking of the shaft of the waterwheel. It will be immediately repaired.

 

Wm. Mason, who has been living in the BlaKeslee house, moved last week to Benjamin Nichol's place, on Five Mile Hill.

 

Miss Lizzie Mason, who has been living at Edwin Tomlinson's, will go to Five Mile Hill, to live at her brother's and attend school.

 

Frank Downs commenced on Monday repairing the roads.

 

Wm. Smith has purchased the house formerly owned by DeWitt Hull, and moved in on Monday.

 

Mrs. Tolles has been a great sufferer for the last ten days from a carbuncle on the back of her neck, but is now somewhat better.

 

OXFORD.

 

Rev. F. E. Snow received a telegram1 on Thursday announcing the death of his mother in St. Louis, Mo., where she had recently gone on a visit to a son.

 

Fifty-two tickets were sold at Towantic on Thursday for the Danbury fair, and the people had to wait about four hours for the special train and then the thirteen or more cars were already crowded. A good number of the people from here went to Southford to take the cars where of course the chances would be less for getting a seat.

 

N. J. Botsford and Joseph Connor of New York City, are making their annual fall sojourn here, with headquarters at the Oxford House.

 

Frederick W. Hubbell having taken possession of the James McEwen place on behalf of the town, Rev. Mr. Bailey has removed to and occupies a portion of the residence of Mrs. W. H. Davis.

 

Rev. Mr. Snow's vacation was cut short by the summons to return and attend the funeral of Mr. Wilcoxson.

 

Nathan J. Warner, grandson of the late Mr. Wilcoxson, has been sick at his grandmother's, Mrs. P. P. Warner, since last Sunday, but is now on the gain.

 

Burritt Davis was relieved of his pocket book at the Danbury fair on Wednesday, but fortunately he did not have a large sum of money with him. Others from here are also reported to have had their pockets picked.

 

There were only 142 votes polled on Monday, and none for or against license. The following were elected to the respective offices:

Selectmen, George R. Baldwin, John R. Pone. Lewis B. Perkins.

Town Clerk and Registrar of Births, Marriages and Deaths, Lewis Barnes.

Town Treasurer and Treasurer of School and Deposit Fund, Charles H. Butler.

Registrar of Voters, Egbert J. Thrall.

Collector of Taxes on list of 1882, Anthony B. Hinman.

Registrar of Voters, Benjamin Nichols.

Assessors, Albert D. Smith, Wales Chatneld.

Board of Relief, William M. Hubbell, Clark E. Lam, Frederick W. Hubbell.

Auditor, Clark E. Lum, John Harger.

School Visitor, Clark E. Lum, Frederick E. Snow.

Grand Jurors, George Lum, Cornelius

C. Rider, John French, Albert D. Smith, Atwater Treat, David B. Wheeler.

Constables, Anthony B. Hinman, LeGrand W. Lake, Dwight Washband, Benjamin C. Pope, Hobart Booth, Henry E. Smith, William O. Davis.

Road Commissioner, William H. Davis.

Justices of the Peace, Benjamin Nichols, Clark E. Lum. William O. French, David Hawley. John Church.
