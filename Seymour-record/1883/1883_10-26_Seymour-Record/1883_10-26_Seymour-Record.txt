1883_10-26_Seymour-Record

OXFORD.

Mrs. Ebenezer Riggs is sick with erysipelas.

There will be services in St. Peter's church next Sunday, both morning and afternoon.

Only one was admitted as an elector by the board on Wednesday, and at the expense of $12.

Mr. E. Hoadley of Seymour has purchased the woodland belonging to the estate of the late Nathan J. Wilcoxson, lying west of and near the turnpike.

Mrs. Roswell Cable is quite poorly, at the residence of her daughter, Mrs. L. L. Leek, and owing to her advanced years is not expected to recover.

The democratic caucus for the nomination of a candidate for representative will be held at the town hall on Saturday evening Nov. 3rd.

John McCrene has gone into the employ of the Telephone Co., as inspector of a line that is being built between Pittsburgh, Pa„ and Buffalo, N. Y.

Mr. A. A. Baker and sister of Huntsville, Ala., are visiting a few days at Mrs. Leek's. Mr. Baker goes into the employ of the Farrel Foundry Co. of Ansonia as a machinist. Miss Baker will return to Huutsville, Ala., in company with Mrs. R. W. Baker, who will not move on till next spring.
