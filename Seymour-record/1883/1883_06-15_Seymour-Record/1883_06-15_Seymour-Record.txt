1883_06-15_Seymour-Record

SQUANTUCK.

The ladies will hold a strawberry and ice cream festival at the house of William Sherman on Great Hill next Thursday evening, the 3lst.  If it should be stormy then the next evening.

Mrs. D. M. Judson and daughter from Stratford have been spending a few days at Mr. Clemens'.

Mrs. Chas. Fogg and baby from Waterbury are visiting at Mrs. James Radcliffe's.

Mrs. Jones is much better and able to sit up some.

Ephraim Smith is quite sick with pneumonia, and is attended by Dr. A. Beardsley from Birmingham.

Mr. Clemons has been having his house newly shingled by parties from Birmingham.

Heury Wheeler has moved away from this place with his family.

Stephen Mansfield is living with Henry Smith this Summer.

Michael Fanning deeded away his homestead about a year ago, and has now brought suit to have the deed set aside.
