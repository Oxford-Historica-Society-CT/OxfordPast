1883_07-06_Seymour-Record

SQUANTUCK.

The wind storm Monday evening blew down a number of large apple trees on Win. C. Smith's place, and blew off a large maple limb at A. C. Hanchet's, nearly demolishing his ice house and barely missing Dr. Beardsley who was passing at the time.

Mrs. Wm. C. Sharpe and Miss Fannie Dibble were visiting friends in this place last Saturday.

Mr. Henry Rose of Oxford and Mrs. Ella Morey of Monroe were married a few days ago by Esq. Hollister of Monroe, and have gone to keeping house in the basement of Geo. W. Smith's house. 

OXFORD.

Messrs. Stuart & Robinson went up to Quassapaug to play. The exercises of the Fourth closed with an oration in the evening.

The subject of Rev. Mr. Bailey's discourse next Sunday afternoon will be the 5th commandment.

The funeral of Mrs. Robert Wheeler was attended from the house and St. Peter's church last Tuesday afternoon.

Mrs. R. W. Baker of Huntsville, Ala., is making her annual visit north, with headquarters at her mother's, Mrs. L. L. Leek.

Officers in search of the New Milford horse thieves changed horses and passed through this place Wednesday evening, and were sanguine that they were close on to the thieves.

The subject of Rev. Mr. Pardee's remarks last Sunday morning was the New Testament version of the First Commandment: "Love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and thy neighbor as thyself."

QUAKER FARMS

The hay crop seems plenteous. Apple trees give promise of an average crop.

One of our neighbors uses 3,000 tins to protect his tobacco plants from cut worms.

Henry E. Bidwell died at his home on Tuesday morning, the 20th ult. Mr. Bidwell had been a great sufferer with cancers at different times, having been to Rome, N. Y., and had 34-cancers removed. He was 79 years old. The funeral was attended on Thursday at 2 p. m.
