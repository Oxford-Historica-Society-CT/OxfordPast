1883_01-19_Seymour-Record

 

OXFORD

 

The Board of Relief will meet on Saturday of this week, the 20th.

 

Mrs. E. J. Alling visited her friends in Schenectady, N.Y., last week.

 

Mrs. L. L. Leek has returned from a visit of several weeks in Ansonia and New Haven.

 

There was a large party in attendance at the initiation of O. C. Osborn's hall on Towantic on Thursday evening of last week.

 

George A. Flagg, clerk in the pension office at Washington, D. C., is recovering from several weeks of malarial fever.

 

Rev. Mr. Snow's parishioners propose paying him a donation visit on Wednesday afternoon and evening of next week.

 

Rev. Mr. Bailey is to deliver the essay at the convocation to be holden in St. Thomas Church, New Haen, on Wednesday, the 24th.

 

A telegraph operator has been assigned, and, in connection with station agent duties, entered upon his work at Towantic.

 

The ladies' socity of St Peter's church et at the residence of the rector on Thursday of last week.

 

C.B. Perry visits the legislature at Hartford and also attends the graduating exercises at the Normal School, New Britain, this week.

 

In addition to the regular morning and afternoon services in St. Peter's church next Sunday, Rev. Mr. Bailey will, if the weather is favorable, hold a supplementary service in the evening, commencing at 7 o'clock.

 

In the copied sketch of the representatives last week, representative Osborn of this town is not credited with as large a majority as entitled. It should have read "forty-four instead of "four." And in this connection we would add, that while the Republican nominee in 1881 was elected by eight majority, the remainder of the Democratic ticket received the usual average majority.

 

Gordon R. Crofut and Henry S. Atwood were the jurors called from this town to the present terms of court in the Carroll murder case. The former was accepted and the latter excused

 

George M. Hine, who moved from this place in 1877, and is now Superintendent of the Whitehouse Shoe Factory, in Poughkeepsie, N. Y., paid a visit here last week.

 

On Saturday of last week a gentleman who claimed to be a Mason, and who gave his name as Burton and belonging in Pennsylvania, whither he was travelling, was found by the roadside near the residence of Wm. H. Clark, where he had fallen in a fit. The stranger was taken to the Oxford House where Dr. Barnes attended him, and when he was in partial condition to resume his journey, W. W. Hughes assisted him on his way a few miles in a sleighride. The stranger was very indignant at the suggestion to be assisted by the town. We understand that this unfortunate man was also taken up the night before on Chestnut tree hill and cared by G. W. Cable.

 

BORN:

HULL - In Oxford, Jan 12th, a daughter to Eugene C. and Irene Hull.
