The War Games of August 1912 were among the largest ever on the east coast.  Here the 10th Calvary is shown moving up Great Hill Road to their campsite in Oxford. The presence of the large group of military men and horses attracted many spectators to the area. (Photo from Oxford Historical Society Collection.)

This view of the 10th Calvary campsite was taken from the area where the parking lot to the Immanuel Lutheran Church is now located.  The view looks across Great Hill to land then owned by John Church, the father of well-drilling company founder Stephen B. Church. (Photo from Oxford Historical Society Collection.)
